Mau‑Mau

A fast, friendly shedding game where the first to ditch all their cards wins. The ancestor of UNO.

Players
2–6
Deck
32 or 52
Time
15–30 min
Difficulty
★ Easy

Be the first player to get rid of all your cards.

Setup

  1. Use a standard 52‑card deck (or a 32‑card German deck — 7s through Aces).
  2. Deal 5 cards to each player.
  3. Place the rest face‑down as the stock pile.
  4. Flip the top card face‑up next to it — that starts the discard pile.
Stock · face‑down
9♥
Discard · face‑up

How to Play

On your turn, you must play one card that matches the top of the discard pile by either:

  • Same suit (e.g. ♥ on ♥), or
  • Same rank (e.g. 7 on 7).
9♥ ← 7♥ 9♣ K♠ On the 9♥: play 7♥ (suit ✓) or 9♣ (rank ✓) — not K♠

If you can't play, draw one card from the stock. If it's playable, you may play it immediately; otherwise, your turn ends.

🔔 The Mau‑Mau rule: When you play your second‑to‑last card, shout “Mau!” When you play your last card, shout “Mau‑Mau!” Forget? Draw 2 cards as a penalty.

Special Cards — the fun part

CardEffect
7Next player draws 2 cards (stacks if they also play a 7 → draws 4, 6, 8…)
8Next player skips their turn
9Reverses direction of play
JWild — change the suit to anything you choose
APlayer gets another turn
ℹ️ Regional variants change which cards do what. Agree before you start!

Scoring (optional)

When someone goes out, others count points of cards still in hand: number cards = face value, J/Q/K = 10 points, A = 11 points. Lowest total after several rounds wins — or just play “first to win 3 rounds.”

Strategy Tips

  • 🎯 Save your Jacks — wild cards are powerful late‑game.
  • 🔥 Stack 7s to dump a big penalty on someone.
  • 👀 Watch what suits opponents avoid — they probably don't have them.
  • ⏰ Don't forget to say Mau! — it's the #1 way people lose.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌ Forgetting to say “Mau” on your second‑to‑last card
  • ❌ Playing a Jack and forgetting to call a new suit
  • ❌ Not stacking a 7 when you have one (you eat the penalty instead!)

Rummy

Match, meld, and discard. One of the world's most popular card game families — easy to learn, deep to master.

Players
2–6
Deck
52 cards
Time
20–45 min
Difficulty
★★ Medium

Be the first to “go out” by melding all your cards into valid sets and runs.

Setup

PlayersCards each
210
3–47
5–66
  1. Deal cards as above.
  2. Remaining cards form the stock, face‑down.
  3. Flip the top card face‑up to start the discard pile.
  4. Ace ranks low (A‑2‑3 is a valid run).

What Counts as a Meld?

🔢 Set (group): three or four cards of the same rank, different suits.

7♠ 7♥ 7♦ ✓ Valid set — same rank, different suits

🔗 Run (sequence): three or more consecutive cards of the same suit.

5♣ 6♣ 7♣ 8♣ ✓ Valid run — consecutive, same suit

How to Play

Each turn has 3 steps:

  1. Draw — take one card from the stock OR the top of the discard pile.
  2. Meld (optional) — lay down any valid set/run, or add cards to existing melds (yours or others, depending on variant).
  3. Discard — place one card face‑up on the discard pile. Turn ends.

When a player has melded all their cards (with one card to discard last), they “go out” and win the round.

Scoring

When someone goes out, opponents score penalty points for cards left in hand:

CardPoints
2–10Face value
J / Q / K10 each
A1 (low) or 15 (high) — agree first

Game often ends when someone reaches 100 or 500 points (loser high) — adjust to taste.

Strategy Tips

  • 🎯 Plan two melds at once — keep cards that could go either way.
  • 🗑️ Watch the discard pile — never feed opponents the card they're collecting.
  • ⚡ Go out fast in early rounds — penalty points add up.
  • 🃏 Don't hoard high cards (J/Q/K) — they're 10 points each if caught.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌ Drawing from the discard pile without a plan
  • ❌ Forgetting to discard at the end of your turn
  • ❌ Melding too early and showing your hand

Popular Variants

  • Gin Rummy — 2 players, 10 cards each, “knock” when unmelded points ≤ 10.
  • Rummy 500 — meld onto opponents' sets; play to 500 points.
  • Canasta — uses 2 decks + jokers, focused on 7‑card “canastas.”
  • Indian Rummy — 13 cards, includes a wildcard joker.

Shithead

A chaotic, hilarious shedding game. Last player holding cards is the “shithead” and deals next round.

Players
2–5
Deck
52 cards
Time
15–30 min
Difficulty
★★ Medium

Get rid of all your cards. Don't be the last one holding any.

Setup

Each player gets 9 cards in three rows:

Face‑down — can't look 🙈
5♦ J♠ 8♥ Face‑up — everyone sees 👀
A♣ 2♥ K♠ Your hand — only you see 🤫
  1. Deal 3 face‑down cards (don't peek!).
  2. Deal 3 face‑up cards on top of those.
  3. Deal 3 cards to each player's hand.
  4. Before play starts, swap any hand cards with your face‑up cards to optimize.
  5. Remaining cards form the draw pile.

How to Play

Play moves clockwise. On your turn, play a card equal to or higher than the top of the discard pile.

  • Always keep 3 cards in hand by drawing from the draw pile after playing.
  • Can't play? Pick up the entire discard pile into your hand. 😱
  • Once the draw pile is empty, play through your face‑up row, then face‑down row (you flip these blind!).

Magic Cards

CardEffect
2Reset — next player can play anything
7Next player must play 7 or lower
8Invisible — next player matches the card under the 8
10Burns the pile — discard it all, same player goes again
4 × same rankBurns the pile (any time, even mid‑stack)
🔥 Burn the pile = it's gone forever. A powerful escape from a high stack.

The Endgame

Once your hand and face‑up cards are gone, you play face‑down cards blind:

  1. Flip one without looking.
  2. If it's playable → you played it!
  3. If it's not → pick up the pile and resume normal play with that card in hand.

Last player still holding cards = the Shithead. 💩 They deal next round (and endure the shame).

Strategy Tips

  • 🎯 Save your 2s, 10s, and Jokers for emergencies (escape big piles).
  • 👀 Watch what's face‑up in front of opponents — they're stuck playing those eventually.
  • 🔥 Burn the pile when it gets high — don't let yourself be forced to pick it up.
  • 🃏 Swap weak hand cards into face‑up before play starts.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌ Looking at your face‑down cards (you can't!)
  • ❌ Wasting 10s early when the pile is small
  • ❌ Forgetting to draw back up to 3 cards

Variants

Also known as Karma, Palace, or President (in some regions). Magic card rules vary wildly — agree before starting!

Hearts

A classic trick‑taking game where you want fewer points, not more. Avoid Hearts and dodge the Queen of Spades!

Players
4 (best)
Deck
52 cards
Time
30–60 min
Difficulty
★★ Medium

Score the fewest points. Game ends when someone hits 100 — lowest score wins.

Penalty Cards

A♥1 pt K♥1 pt Q♥ J♥ …×13 Q♠13! Each ♥ = 1 point · Q♠ = 13 points 😱 · everything else = 0

Setup

  1. Deal all 52 cards — 13 to each player.
  2. Pass 3 cards to another player before play starts. The direction rotates each round:
Round 1
⬅️
Pass left
Round 2
➡️
Pass right
Round 3
⬆️
Pass across
Round 4
✋
No pass

…then the cycle repeats.

How to Play

  1. The player with the 2 of Clubs leads it to start the first trick.
  2. Going clockwise, each player plays one card. You must follow suit if possible.
  3. If you can't follow suit, play any card (this is your chance to dump Hearts or the ♠Q).
  4. Highest card of the led suit wins the trick.
  5. Winner of the trick leads the next one.
🚫 You cannot lead Hearts until Hearts have been “broken” — meaning, until someone has played one because they couldn't follow suit.

Shoot the Moon!

If you collect ALL 13 Hearts + the Queen of Spades in one round, instead of 26 points to you: ✨ all other players get 26 points each. ✨ A massive risk‑reward play — usually you should try to block someone you suspect of shooting.

Scoring

After each round, total each player's penalty cards. The game ends when any player reaches 100 points. Lowest score wins.

Strategy Tips

  • 🗡️ Pass dangerous cards — high spades (A, K, Q), high hearts, voids in a suit you want to dump.
  • 🎯 Try to be “void” in a suit early so you can dump hearts/♠Q later.
  • 👑 Watch for the Queen of Spades — if you have it, play it on the highest spade trick you can.
  • 🌙 Spot the moon shoot — if one player is taking every heart, you may need to let them win the ♠Q to break it.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌ Leading Hearts before they're broken
  • ❌ Holding onto the Queen of Spades when you should be dumping her
  • ❌ Forgetting which direction to pass

Spades

A team‑based trick‑taking game built around bidding. Promise tricks, deliver them, win big.

Players
4 (2×2 teams)
Deck
52 cards
Time
30–60 min
Difficulty
★★★ Hard

First team to 500 points wins. Spades are always trump. ♠️

Setup

  1. Partners sit across from each other:
🧑Team A
🧑Team B
♠
🧑Team B
🧑Team A
  1. Deal all 52 cards — 13 each.
  2. Cards rank: A (high) → K → Q → J → 10 → … → 2 (low).
  3. ♠️ Spades always beat every other suit.

Bidding — the key skill

Before play, each player declares how many tricks they'll take (0 through 13).

  • A team's bid = sum of both partners' bids.
  • A bid of 0 = “Nil” — partner risks more for a big bonus (see below).

How to Play

  1. The player left of the dealer leads any non‑Spade card (Spades are “not broken” yet).
  2. Going clockwise, each player must follow suit if able.
  3. Can't follow? Play any card — including a Spade to “trump.”
  4. Highest Spade wins. If no Spades were played, highest card of the led suit wins.
  5. Winner leads the next trick.
🚫 You can't lead Spades until someone has trumped a trick (Spades are “broken”).

Scoring

  • ✅ Made your bid: 10 × bid points, plus 1 point per overtrick (“bag”).
  • ❌ Missed your bid: −10 × bid points (negative!).
  • 🛍️ Bags penalty: every 10 bags accumulated = −100 points. Overtricks hurt long‑term!
  • 🌟 Nil bonus: successful Nil (took 0 tricks) = +100; failed Nil (took any trick) = −100.

Blind Nil (advanced)

Bid Nil before looking at your cards — for ±200 points. A high‑risk classic.

Strategy Tips

  • 🎯 Count tricks carefully — overbidding tanks you, underbidding builds bags.
  • 🤝 Read your partner's bid — adjust yours to balance.
  • ♠️ Save spades for stealing tricks opponents lead.
  • 🚮 Dump high cards when your partner is winning a trick — don't waste them.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌ Overbidding to “look good” — the penalty is harsh
  • ❌ Forgetting bags accumulate across rounds
  • ❌ Leading spades too early — illegal until broken

Go Fish

The perfect starter card game. Ask for cards, collect sets of 4, try not to go “fishing.”

Players
2–6
Deck
52 cards
Time
10–20 min
Difficulty
★ Easy

Collect the most “books” — sets of 4 cards of the same rank.

Setup

PlayersCards each
2–37
4–65

Remaining cards go face‑down in the middle as the “pond.”

How to Play

  1. Pick a player and ask: “Do you have any sevens?” (or any rank). 🛑 You must already hold at least one card of that rank.
  2. If they have any → they give you ALL of them → you go again.
  3. If they don't → they say “Go Fish!” → you draw one card from the pond. 🎣 If you drew the rank you asked for, show it and go again. Otherwise, your turn ends.

Whenever you collect all 4 of a rank, lay them face‑up as a book in front of you.

Game End

The game ends when all 13 books have been collected, or the pond runs out and players empty their hands. Most books wins!

Strategy Tips

  • 🧠 Remember what others ask for — they have those ranks.
  • 🎯 Target players who recently asked for cards you might want later.
  • 🤫 Don't reveal more than needed — answer just yes or no.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌ Asking for a rank you don't already hold (illegal!)
  • ❌ Forgetting to “go again” after a successful match
👶 Great for: kids, beginners, and warming up before a longer game night.

The original UNO. Match suit or rank, dump your hand, and use 8s as wild cards.

2–7
Deck

Time

★ Easy

Setup

  2. Place the rest face‑down as the stock.
  3. Flip the top card to start the discard pile. If it's an 8, bury it and flip another.

How to Play

On your turn, play one card that matches the top of the discard pile by:

  • Same suit, or
  • Same rank, or
  • Any 8 (wild — see below).

Can't play? Draw from stock until you can (some house rules: just draw 1).

⭐ The Crazy 8: when you play an 8, declare a new suit. The next player must play that suit (or another 8).

War

The simplest card game ever. Pure luck. Great with kids — or when you're too tired to think.

Players
2
Deck
52 cards
Time
10–30 min
Difficulty
★ Easy

Win all 52 cards. Or, more realistically, play until someone gives up.

Setup

  1. Split the deck evenly — 26 cards each, face‑down.
  2. Don't look at them.

How to Play

Both players flip the top card of their pile at the same time:

  • 🏆 Higher card wins both cards. Put them at the bottom of your stack.
  • ⚔️ Tie? It's WAR!

How war works:

  1. Each player places 3 cards face‑down.
  2. Then flips a 4th card face‑up.
  3. Higher face‑up card wins all 10 cards in the pile.
  4. Another tie? Another war (3 down, 1 up) — stack the prizes!
4♦ vs J♠ J♠ is higher → B wins both cards
Q♥ vs Q♣ Tie → WAR! ⚔️ Each plays 3 down + 1 up: 9♠ vs K♦ K♦ beats 9♠ → B wins all 10 cards

Game End

The game ends when one player has all 52 cards.

⚠️ Games can drag on forever. Common fix: play to a time limit, or “whoever has more cards after 15 minutes.”

“Strategy” Tips

There is no strategy. It's 100% luck. Embrace it.

  • 🎲 Cheer dramatically on every flip
  • ⚔️ War is the fun part — make it loud
👶 Great for: kids learning card ranks, killing 10 minutes, or anyone who just wants to flip cards without thinking.

Blackjack

Beat the dealer by getting as close to 21 as possible — without going over.

Players
2–7 + dealer
Deck
1–8 decks
Time
20–60 min
Difficulty
★★ Medium

Get a hand value closer to 21 than the dealer — without going over (busting).

Card Values

CardValue
2–10Face value
J / Q / K10
A1 or 11 (you choose)

A hand with an Ace counted as 11 is called a “soft” hand (it can't bust on the next card).

Setup

  1. One player is the dealer (rotate or fix).
  2. Players place bets (chips, candy, anything).
  3. The dealer gives each player 2 cards face‑up.
  4. The dealer takes 2 cards — one face‑up, one face‑down (“hole card”).
A♠ K♥ = 21 🌟 You: Ace + ten‑value = Blackjack! 10♦ Dealer: one up, one face‑down (“hole card”)
🌟 Blackjack! Got an Ace + a 10‑value card in your first two cards? That's a Blackjack — instant win, paid 3:2, unless the dealer also has one (push).

How to Play

Going around the table, each player picks one option per turn:

ActionWhat it does
HitTake another card
StandKeep your hand, end turn
Double DownDouble your bet, take exactly one more card
SplitIf your two cards match rank, split into two hands (extra bet on the second)
Surrender (optional)Forfeit half your bet, end the hand
💥 Go over 21? You BUST. You lose immediately, regardless of the dealer.

The Dealer's Rules

After all players act, the dealer flips the hole card and plays by fixed rules:

  • Hand value 16 or less → must hit.
  • Hand value 17 or more → must stand.
  • Some tables: dealer hits on soft 17.

The dealer has no choices — just follows the rules.

How You Win

OutcomeResult
Your hand > dealer (no bust)✅ Win 1:1
Blackjack vs. non‑blackjack dealer✅ Win 3:2
Tie🤝 Push (bet returned)
Your hand < dealer (or you bust)❌ Lose bet
Dealer busts, you didn't✅ Win 1:1

Basic Strategy — the smart play

A simplified cheat sheet:

HitStandDoubleSplit
Your handDealer shows 2–6Dealer shows 7–A
8 or lessHitHit
9–11Double DownHit
12–16StandHit
17+StandStand
A,ASplitSplit
8,8SplitSplit
10,10StandStand
🎯 The golden rule: assume the dealer's hole card is a 10.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌ Taking insurance (it's a sucker bet)
  • ❌ Standing on soft 17 (you can't bust — hit!)
  • ❌ Splitting 10s (you've already got 20, don't ruin it)
  • ❌ Chasing losses with bigger bets

Poker — Texas Hold'em

The world's most popular poker variant. Two hole cards, five community cards, four betting rounds. Easy to learn, lifetime to master.

Players

Setup

  1. One player has the Dealer button (rotates clockwise each hand).
  2. The player left of the dealer posts the small blind (forced bet).
  3. The next player posts the big blind (2× small blind).
  4. Each player gets 2 cards face‑down (“hole cards”).

The Four Betting Rounds

  1. Pre‑Flop — after hole cards are dealt. Starting left of the big blind, each player can fold (quit the hand), call (match the current bet), or raise (increase the bet).
  2. The Flop — dealer puts 3 community cards face‑up in the middle. New betting round.
  3. The Turn — dealer adds a 4th community card. New betting round.
  4. The River — dealer adds a 5th and final community card. Final betting round.

The Showdown

If 2+ players remain, they reveal cards. Best 5‑card hand (made from any combination of the 2 hole + 5 community cards) wins the pot. If only one player remains (everyone else folded), they win — and don't have to show their cards.

A♠ K♠ Your hole cards
Q♠ J♠ 10♠ 3♦ 7♣ Community cards — best 5 of all 7: A♠ K♠ Q♠ J♠ 10♠ = Royal Flush 👑

Starting Hand Strength — rough guide

TierHands
🔥 PremiumAA, KK, QQ, AK
💪 StrongJJ, 10‑10, AQ, AJ, KQ
👍 Playable99–22, suited connectors (JT, T9), Ax suited
😬 MarginalEverything else — fold most

Strategy Tips

  • 🎯 Play tight early — only strong starting hands (pairs, A‑K, A‑Q, suited connectors).
  • 👀 Position matters — acting last gives you info on others.
  • 🧮 Pot odds — calculate if a call is worth it vs. the pot size.
  • 🃏 Bluff sparingly — works best against thoughtful opponents.
  • 😶 Don't tilt — bad beats happen; play the math, not the emotion.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌ Playing too many hands (“any 2 cards can win” — they can, but rarely)
  • ❌ Calling instead of raising with strong hands
  • ❌ Bluffing players who always call
  • ❌ Chasing draws with bad pot odds

Other Poker Variants

  • Omaha — 4 hole cards, must use exactly 2.
  • Seven‑Card Stud — no community cards, 7 cards each.
  • Five‑Card Draw — classic home‑game poker, draw to replace cards.
